No Van·i·ty
/ˈnō - vanədē/
noun
1. To not have excessive pride in or admiration of one's own appearance or achievements.
"His modesty shone through as he took compliments with no vanity."
2. The quality of being meaningful or purposeful.
"She exemplified the essence of no vanity in her actions and words."
